Cyber Threats Are Increasing ‚Äì Ransomware and malware attacks can wipe out critical business data in minutes. A redundant backup solution ensures you have a clean copy to restore.

Natural Disasters & Unforeseen Events ‚Äì Floods, fires, and power outages can render local data storage useless. Off-site and cloud-based redundancy solutions keep your business operational.

Stacking this with all the extra tasks business owners have to manage themselves, like payroll, HR, accounting, marketing, and sales, not to mention operating in the business to fulfill what the company needs to keep the lights on.

If it's broken, let's fix it. That's the saying, right?!

Now that we know what to avoid and the plethora of ways business owners can drop the ball, it's time to talk about the sexy solution of redundancy. It's easy to say, just set up a backup, but let's get specific. Not every business is the same. It's easier to say every company is a unique snowflake.

Let's cover some different options based on what you can start with, and at the very least, have a jumping-off point, Like:

Encryption is one of the most potent tools in cybersecurity, yet many small business owners and employees don't fully understand how or why they should use it. Whether it's sending an email, a message in a chat, or sending a file, Encryption scrambles data in transit so that only the intended recipient can read it, protecting sensitive information from cybercriminals wanting to intercept your message or text along the way.

So now we have a hammer in our hands; we're looking for nails. Let's list some places we can find them:

Email: Knowing why you would and wouldn't encrypt an email is something many professionals don't know, and maybe they should.
Cloud storage can be scary as you use a platform with unknown security risks. So, now, imagine you're uploading banking information on this platform. Yep, I'm scared too. It's an accident waiting to happen. Encrypting the file before you upload it will ensure it gets to the platform safely.
Chat platforms: Apps like WhatsApp, iMessage, or Signal have 'end-to-end encryption.' It's extra safe.



Why don't we use it if it's a matter of not getting hacked?
It's simple to hear a bad story about someone who sent an email and didn't tick a box or save a file in the right spot, leaving a vulnerable file on a computer, and BAM! They got hacked. But it wasn't you, so we don't care.
