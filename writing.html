Clarity In Climbing

How do we know we are climbing the right hill?

May 28, 2025 Me

Countless articles, ads, videos, blogs, and social media posts flood our search results to entice us to click a link. Whether it's running a marathon, earning a million dollars, getting a six pack, writing a book, owning your own business, or earning passive income in X amount of days. Clickbait articles are easy to find and aren't as helpful as we might think.

If you've been down these rabbit holes before, like I have, you'll know. It takes careful filtering to find reputable content. But once you find all the information you'll ever need, you'll soon find out knowing what to do is the easy part.

I've been caught on this loop for years. I learn something to help myself. I feel smart because I understand it and then, I keep looking for more information to help me. You see the problem, don't you? We can learn new things endlessly from our couch, but nothing gets done. Aristotle said it best. "The purpose of knowledge is action, not knowledge."

Doing the work and being patient while you see no results for years is really hard. No matter what hill you're climbing, they all seem incredibly steep, with no end sight, and the path won't be recognizable until it's under your feet. So if we understand, we should be working really hard to get what we want to. How do we know we are climbing the right hill? How do we know our efforts won't be wasted?

Will we just have to start again from scratch?

I've thought about this for years. I would argue this fear feels real, but doesn't make sense. Let me explain. Simply put, you climb. You work hard. If you fail and realize you've been working hard on the wrong thing. You've now gained clarity. You've practiced working hard. There's only upside here. Starting from scratch doesn't exist. Failing equals learning, not failing.
Furthermore, I've experienced upside and been living example of learning, not failing. For instance, I am a professional musician, qualified tool maker, sales professional, working in IT for the last five years, who loves fitness, writing and learning Russian. I've tried many careers. Climbed many hills and started from scratch, many times - I've practiced quitting plenty. I've gained clarity knowing what I want, because I've tried it, I'm never left wondering. Had I stayed in one industry, I would've gained experience in the industry for 20 years. But if I stayed in music, I guarantee I wouldn't have the skills that I have now.

Clarity comes to those who climb and quit for the right reasons. Only you can know how hard you tried. This involves a level of honesty with yourself. Let me ask you this. What do people do when they feel like they're playing a zero sum game? They complain. Have you ever noticed how often people complain? A lot, right? This is the signal to open your eyes to the kind of people you spend your time with. We know complaining doesn't help. We know there's a value in quitting, and we know climbing the mountain that feels steep with unknown terrain is inevitable. The problem to solve ends up being. How do we know we are climbing the right mountain?

I've got some ideas on how to speed up the process:

  1. Write down everything you're experiencing as you're going through it, formalize your thoughts. This automatically calls you out on your own bullshit. I know this because it's worked for me.
  2. Find someone who is further along and ask for help. Asking for help doesn't mean what you think it means. Every successful business owner asks for help every day to keep their business thriving and if you have genuine energy about your approach, people love to help.
  3. Track your progress. This will help you stay accountable for simple and important metrics you want to hit. Most issues can be solved with data and math, but if you don't track anything, you'll always feel like you're spinning your wheels. Without touching the road.
Okay, so how do we keep climbing? Here are some ways that keep me engaged:
  • Read the articles which interest you, and if you hear something that is a seemingly good idea, test it for yourself, put it to action.
  • Treat information from experts with skepticism.
  • Track your progress and remind yourself of your progress. Put it in the calendar. Do what you need to do to get it in front of you.
  • Share your process out loud on social media.
  • Find like-minded friends to lean on. Don't quit. There are too many people in the world quitting on their dreams.
More on this last point, and this is just me here, but I'd rather be known for someone who managed to reach their dream at age 60. Then give up at age 30 and never have the chance because I'm a realist. Consider this. Think about having a problem for 30 years now. Do you think you have enough time to solve it?

Patience is the game here.

You don't have to solve your problem straight away. You have time to just keep climbing. As you go through different seasons in your life, you'll be able to move quicker in some seasons compared to others. Your environment plays a huge factor in operating at your best. This is also the reason an article or a blog post can only take you so far. Why? an article can't take your environment into account to give you the perfect answer. An article also can't get your ass off the couch and do anything for you. Don't get me wrong, these posts aren't bad or pointless. They are the seed of the idea for you to test, try and discard if you continuously keep this cycle in your life. You are bound to find something. You would've practiced enough good ideas to be good at testing ideas, and something will stick. Something useful will stick, and you'll be better for it.

Hopefully this paints the picture for you to keep climbing, try and test new ideas, but do something. Don't stop at just reading for years like I did without doing anything.

It doesn't get you anywhere. Take care.
